Welcome to Office Forests

A Johnson Controls Tech Challenge entry from the Techn Comms department

The problem...

In their seminal work Biophilic Design: The Theory, Science, and Practice of Bringing Buildings to Life (2008), Stephen R. Kellert, and Judith Heerwagen, and Martin Mador explore how integrating natural elements into architectural design can enhance human well-being and productivity: "We will never be truly healthy, satisfied, or fulfilled if we live apart and alienated from the environment from which we evolved".

Johnson Controls helps office buildings become greener in terms of energy efficiency, but what if we could help them become greener in a more literal sense as well? Modern offices now have green walls, green external envelopes, green dividers, green ceilings, and more plants than ever before. However, building management systems do not take plants into account when delivering clean air to building spaces, even though plants are miniature filtration systems. Furthermore, enterprise management systems do not take office plants into account in their sustainability metrics.

The solution...

What is the most effective way to make the building aware of the plants within it, without costly sensors in each plant or time-consuming in person visits?

Office Forests builds on the OpenBlue Companion app to gather this data through images. An optional prompt within the app suggests building occupants photo a tree near their desk. The location of the image is noted automatically on the Companion floor and the plant is identified through a computer vision model. The user gets information on the plant type, origin, and health benefits, while the location and identification are shared with OpenBlue Enterprise Manager, which over time builds up a database of the plants within a building. This data enriches the building's sustainability metrics and can also be shared with BMS systems, which can make adjustments to air turnover based on the assumption that trees remove CO2 at defined rates.

Peace Lily

The peace lily (Spathiphyllum) is a popular plant known for its elegant white flowers and lush green leaves. It originates from the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in the warm, humid environment. Peace lilies are not only beautiful but also excellent air purifiers, making them a favorite choice for indoor gardening.

  • Air Purification: Peace lilies are excellent at removing harmful toxins such as formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene from the air, which can improve indoor air quality and reduce symptoms like headaches and respiratory issues.
  • Humidity Regulation: Peace lilies help maintain optimal humidity levels by releasing moisture into the air. This can prevent dry skin and respiratory problems, making the office environment more comfortable12.
  • Care: Prefers indirect light and consistently moist soil.

Spider Plant

The spider plant (Chlorophytum comosum) is a houseplant known for its arching green and white striped leaves and its ability to produce numerous offshoots, or "spiderettes." Native to the coastal regions of South Africa, spider plants thrive in a variety of indoor conditions, making them a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.

  • Health benefits: Spider plants are excellent at removing pollutants such as formaldehyde, xylene, and toluene from the air, which can enhance indoor air quality and reduce symptoms like headaches and respiratory issues.
  • Care: Prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings.

Monstera deliciosa

Monstera deliciosa, also known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a popular tropical houseplant native to Central America. It is recognized for its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive splits and holes. This climbing evergreen thrives in warm, humid environments and can grow up to 15 feet tall indoors

  • Health benefits: helps filter out pollutants like formaldehyde and benzene, improving indoor air quality.
  • Care: prefers bright, indirect sunlight. Water moderately when the top inch of soil dries out. Thrives in high humidity; mist the leaves regularly.

    • Dracaena

    Dracaena is a genus of over 120 species of trees and shrubs known for their striking, sword-like leaves and hardy nature. These plants are native to Africa, southern Asia, and northern Australia, and are popular as low-maintenance houseplants. Dracaenas can vary significantly in size, from small shrubs to tall trees, and are often used for their decorative foliage.

    • Health benefits: effective at removing toxins like formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene from the air, improving indoor air quality.
    • Care: prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate low light conditions. Water when the top inch of soil is dry; avoid overwatering. Benefits from higher humidity levels; mist the leaves occasionally.

    Ficus benjamina

    Ficus benjamina, commonly known as the Weeping Fig, is a popular houseplant recognized for its graceful, arching branches and glossy, evergreen leaves. Native to Southeast Asia and Australia, this plant can grow up to 6 feet tall indoors and is often used to add a touch of elegance to home and office spaces. It is known for its resilience and ability to adapt to various indoor environments.

  • Health benefits: helps remove pollutants like formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene from the air, improving indoor air quality.
  • Care: prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate some direct sunlight. Water when the top inch of soil is dry; avoid overwatering. Thrives in high humidity; mist the leaves regularly.

    • Howea forsteriana

    Howea forsteriana, commonly known as the Kentia Palm, is a slow-growing palm native to Lord Howe Island in Australia. It is prized for its elegant, arching fronds and slender trunk, making it a popular choice for indoor and office environments. This palm can grow up to 10 meters tall in ideal conditions but typically reaches about 3 meters indoors.

    • Health benefits: helps remove toxins such as benzene, formaldehyde, and trichloroethylene from the air, improving indoor air quality.
    • Care: prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate low light conditions. Water when the top inch of soil is dry; avoid overwatering. Thrives in moderate to high humidity; mist the leaves occasionally.

    Searsia lancea

    Searsia lancea, commonly known as the Karee or African Sumac, is an evergreen tree native to South Africa. It is known for its graceful, weeping form and dark green, trifoliate leaves. This drought-resistant tree can grow up to 8 meters tall and is often used for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

  • Health benefits: helps filter out pollutants, improving indoor air quality.
  • Care: thrives in full sun to partial shade. Drought-tolerant; water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings. Prune as needed to maintain shape and remove dead or diseased branches.

    • Aloe Vera

    Aloe vera, also known as Aloe barbadensis, is a succulent plant species with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Native to arid regions of Africa, it is now cultivated worldwide for its medicinal and cosmetic uses. Aloe vera is renowned for its soothing gel, which is used to treat burns, skin irritations, and other ailments.

    • Health benefits: Aloe vera helps remove toxins like formaldehyde and benzene from the air, improving indoor air quality. The gel from its leaves can be used to soothe minor burns and skin irritations, providing a natural first-aid solution.
    • Care: prefers bright, indirect sunlight; too much direct sunlight can cause the leaves to turn yellow. Water sparingly; allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings.

    Pothos

    Pothos (Epipremnum aureum), also known as Devil's Ivy, is a popular houseplant known for its trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves. Native to the Solomon Islands, this tropical plant is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of indoor conditions. Pothos is particularly valued for its low maintenance and air-purifying qualities.

  • Health benefits: helps remove indoor pollutants like formaldehyde, benzene, and carbon monoxide, improving air quality.
  • Care: Prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate low light conditions. Allow the soil to dry out between waterings; avoid overwatering. Thrives in moderate humidity; mist the leaves occasionally..
